Methodology & Guidance Manual

The pathogen prioritisation model bridges the gap between a generalized national view of public-health risk and local, context-specific site pressures. Rather than applying a single uniform monitoring rule, the tool allows local operators, regulators, and scientists to model how pathogen risks shift based on water quality data and environmental pressures.

Quick Start Instructions

1

2
Adjust site levers: Modify local context indicators (0 for absent, 5 for dominant) based on the site's environment.
3
Configure weights: Modify national hazard criteria weights if there is local scientific justification to do so.
4

Core Math Blend Formula

The model uses three distinct scores: a National Hazard Score (calculated from the 5 national weights), a Site Relevance Score (calculated from the 6 site levers), and a Final Blended Score.

Final Score = (National Score * (1 - Site Influence)) + (Site Relevance Score * Site Influence)

At 0% Site Influence, the model acts as a purely national public-health table (ranking hazards solely by exposure efficiency, severity, persistence, shedding, and epidemiological confidence). At 100% Site Influence, the model behaves as a pure site-fit tool, prioritizing hazards solely based on local physical vectors and indicator levels.
